The Structure

How can there be structure when the story jumps all over the place? The film can be analyzed through two screenwriting models: the three-act structure and the 15-beat sheet.

The three-act structure, popularized in 1979 by Syd Field, divides a script into three acts: The Setup, which introduces the protagonist and the inciting incident; The Confrontation, which shows the protagonist repeatedly trying to resolve the issue; and The Solution, which brings the protagonist to the climax and the final resolution. The Daniels put their personal spin and renamed the three acts as: Everything, Everywhere, and All at Once.

The three-act structure includes the 15-beat sheet, which was popularized by screenwriter Blake Snyder in 2005. This structure further divides the three acts into 15 beats, with each beat representing a critical turning point that transforms the protagonist.

The Daniels infuse these Western-style structures with a Japanese storytelling concept called Kishōtenketsu.
