CONCLUSION

There are several reasons why Everything Everywhere All at Once has become a breakout hit. Not only is the story mind-blowingly multilayered, but the low-tech production and Daniels' style set it apart from big Hollywood productions. The film explores Asian American identity, represents our overloaded culture, and was released during a tumultuous pandemic, making it feel timely and original.

It's no surprise that the film became the most awarded of all time, and it even made Oscars history with Michelle Yeoh becoming the first Asian woman to win Best Actress and Ke Huy Quan becoming the second Asian man to win Best Supporting Actor.

There are endless ways to dissect and explore Everything Everywhere All at Once, and watching it once simply won't be enough. Maybe with multiple viewings, you'll discover something you didn't notice before every time.
