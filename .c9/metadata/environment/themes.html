"," How do we find meaning in a meaningless chaotic existence? In an"," attempt to answer the question, Daniels explore four core themes"," in the film: nihilism, absurdism, generational trauma, and"," generational love.","

"," "," Nihilism"," ","

"," ","
"," Nihilism"," is a philosophy that life is meaningless. In the film, Joy"," becomes exhausted with Evelyn's inability to pay attention."," When Evelyn stops Joy from introducing her girlfriend,"," Becky, to Gong Gong, Joy reaches her breaking point and"," becomes Jobu Tupacki. Jobu wants to find the \"right\" Evelyn"," who shares her dissatisfaction with life and is willing to"," kill anyone who stands in her way. Ultimately, she shows"," Evelyn her \"Everything Bagel,\" a tool of self-destruction"," that sucks everything into a pit of oblivion. "," Absurdism"," is the belief that, even though our world is meaningless,"," people can still create a meaningful life. Throughout the"," film, Waymond fully embraces absurdism using an unlikely"," superpower that holds the family together amidst the chaos:"," kindness. "," "," Generational Trauma"," ","

"," ","
"," A core theme of the movie is generational trauma caused by"," high expectations and a lack of empathy between Gong Gong,"," Evelyn, and Joy."," In a flashback of young Evelyn’s life, we hear Gong Gong"," say, “If you abandon this family for that silly boy, then"," we will abandon you. You are not my daughter anymore”"," (0:14:24). This moment shapes the rest of their"," relationship."," Decades later, Evelyn still tries to please Gong Gong"," despite his constant disapproval like “Hmph. Another"," laundromat” (0:12:41) or “Little girl. Always running away."," Never finishing what you started” (0:25:56). Since this was"," the only example of parenting and familial love she grew up"," with, she treats Waymond and Joy with the same emotional"," distance she’s familiar with.","
