ABOUT

","

This project was nearly my everything everywhere for five months between January through May 2023 as part of my thesis for Parsons’ Master in Data Visualization program. This project was especially meaningful to me as a second generation Chinese-American. Everything Everywhere All at Once is the only film that has fully understood my experiences. To see the film being celebrated around the world while working on this project has been a joy! If you’d like to collaborate together, please email me at angelica.hom@gmail.com.


","

DATA AND METHODOLOGY

","

The data for this project was manually collected. The first priority was transcribing and timestamping every line of the film. The second priority was collecting metadata: which character was speaking and to whom, duration of the line, language spoken, which universe and location the line took place, and a sentiment analysis of each line with its own score based on xx. The last priority was outlining the 3-act and 15-beat structure of the film, then categorizing each line to an act and a beat.

THE CHARACTERS

","
One of the unique features of the characters is that they were written to be three-dimensional people instead of just being “good” or “bad”.","

In an interview with Script Apart podcast, Daniel Kwan explains, “We wanted to make sure there were no moral judgements in this film...the fact that we exist in this chaos...it’s enough to cause any well-intentioned person to hurt each other. So the opening is also just filled with these micro-aggressions. There’s like tiny moments of careless racism, careless sexism, careless ageism. It’s all in there and it’s not because any of these people are bad people, it’s because they don’t have the time to fully look at each other and say, ‘You’re a human being who contains multitudes.’”

Everything Everywhere All at Once is a difficult film to describe in just a few words. It’s simultaneously an action-comedy-drama designed to mimic the dizzying pace of our internet-driven society. It speaks to immigrant parents, children of immigrants, queer folks, and those grappling with mental health challenges.
","
At its heart, \"Everything Everywhere All at Once\" is an impressive maximalist cinematic experience but it can be hard to follow the main plot in just one viewing. What do bagels and googly eyes mean? Why do we cry at rocks talking to each other?
","
This project is for fans (like myself) who want a guide to make sense of the film’s chaos. Every line of the film has been transcribed, timestamped, and analyzed. Breaking from traditionally written film analysis, this project embraces a visual data-driven approach focusing on the characters, the themes, the structure, and the universes. Ultimately, I hope it gives fans a deeper appreciation for this bizarre, complex, wonderful film!
