Everything Everywhere All at Once is a difficult film to describe in just a few words. It’s simultaneously an action-comedy-drama designed to mimic the dizzying pace of our internet-driven society. It speaks to immigrant parents, children of immigrants, queer folks, and those grappling with mental health challenges.
\n
At its heart, \"Everything Everywhere All at Once\" is an impressive maximalist cinematic experience but it can be hard to follow the main plot in just one viewing. What do bagels and googly eyes mean? Why do we cry at rocks talking to each other?
\n
This project is for fans (like myself) who want a guide to make sense of the film’s chaos. Every line of the film has been transcribed, timestamped, and analyzed. Breaking from traditionally written film analysis, this project embraces a visual data-driven approach focusing on the characters, the themes, the structure, and the universes. Ultimately, I hope it gives fans a deeper appreciation for this bizarre, complex, wonderful film!
